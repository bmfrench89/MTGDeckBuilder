# The Plan
Flood the board with Spiders, then let **Cosmic Spider-Man** turn them all into
evasive lifelinking threats and swing for the win. Every Spider that enters is a
future attacker; Cosmic is the "on" switch that ends the game in one or two turns.

# How it wins
- Build width with token makers (**Silk, Web Weaver**; **Spiders-Man, Heroic Horde**).
- On your turn, Cosmic gives *all your other Spiders* flying, first strike, trample,
  lifelink, and haste **during combat** — so a wide-but-small board suddenly flies over
  and gains you a pile of life while it connects.
- **Roaming Throne** (naming *Spider*) doubles Cosmic's trigger and your Spiders' ETBs.
- **Kindred Discovery** turns every Spider you play or attack with into a card — this is
  your engine; protect it.

# Mulligan for
- 2+ lands that fix multiple colors (Command Tower, a Triome-style tapland, a fetch),
- at least one early Spider or a mana rock. You do **not** need Cosmic in the opener —
  he's the finisher, not the setup.

# Sequencing tips
- Hold Cosmic until you have 3+ other Spiders and a way to protect him
  (**Heroic Intervention**, **Lightning Greaves**). Alpha-striking into an open board is
  how you lose your team to one wrath.
- Lifelink from Cosmic means you can attack into bigger boards and still race — use it.
- **Web-slinging** cards (like Spiders-Man) are cheaper to "hard cast" early and better
  value late; pay the alternative cost when you want the tokens.

# Weaknesses to respect
- **Board wipes.** Your whole plan is on the battlefield. Keep a protection spell up and
  don't overcommit past a second wipe. Consider adding Teferi's Protection (buy-list).
- **Five colors on a thin green base.** This build leans on rainbow lands + green ramp to
  fix. Prioritize fixing early; a color-screwed Spider deck does nothing.
- **Flying blockers / reach.** Cosmic gives trample to punch through, but a well-timed
  fog or mass-removal can stall you — hold up interaction when you can.
